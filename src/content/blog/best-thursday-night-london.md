## Thursday Is the New Friday (And Has Been for Years)

There's a reason Mayfair insiders rate Thursday above Friday and Saturday. The crowd is different. People who go out on Thursday are genuinely excited to be out — they've chosen to start their weekend early, and that energy is contagious.

On a Saturday, half the crowd is there because it's Saturday and that's what you do. On a Thursday, everyone is there because they want to be. That distinction changes everything.

## Why Thursday Wins

### The Crowd Is Better

Thursday attracts a mix of Londoners who know the scene and international visitors making the most of their trip. The vibe is sociable and energetic without the sometimes-aggressive peak-weekend energy.

### Nearly Everything Is Open

Unlike Monday through Wednesday, Thursday sees nearly every major Mayfair club open its doors. You have options — from exclusive members clubs to high-energy dance floors.

### The Atmosphere Is More Intimate

Even the biggest clubs feel slightly more intimate on a Thursday. The dance floor isn't at crushing capacity. You can actually move. Conversations are possible. The experience feels more personal.

### Getting In Is Easier

Door policies are enforced — always — but Thursday is more forgiving than Saturday. Guestlist works smoothly, walk-ups are more viable, and the queues are shorter.

### Tables Are Easier to Book

Weekend tables sell out fast. Thursday tables are more available, which means better positioning and more flexibility for last-minute plans.

## Where to Go This Thursday

### For Exclusivity: [Tape London](/clubs/tape-london)

Thursday at Tape is more intimate than the weekend. The room is smaller, the crowd is tighter, and the vibe is impeccable. If you can get a table, Thursday at Tape is arguably the best night in Mayfair.

### For Fashion & Cool: [TABU London](/clubs/tabu-london)

TABU's Thursday night is one of the best in the area. The fashion crowd comes out, the music is on point, and the Japanese underground atmosphere is at its moodiest and best.

### For a Party: [Funky Buddha](/clubs/funky-buddha)

Funky Buddha is a Mayfair institution that knows how to deliver a Thursday night. The crowd skews stylish, the music hits hard, and the dance floor has a loose, celebratory energy that makes midweek feel like the weekend.

### For a Show: [Reign London](/clubs/reign-london)

Thursday at Reign London is slightly more intimate than Saturday but the production value is identical. Aerial acts, live performances, and a grand atmosphere.

### For History: [Scotch of St James](/clubs/scotch-of-st-james)

Thursday at Scotch is particularly good. The intimate room fills up nicely, the crowd is elegant, and the atmosphere has a quality that's harder to find on busier nights.

### For House Music: [Maddox](/clubs/maddox)

Start with dinner, transition to house music downstairs. Thursday at Maddox has a more relaxed, sophisticated crowd than the weekend rush.

### For Two Vibes: [Cuckoo Club](/clubs/cuckoo-club)

House downstairs, hip-hop up. Thursday at Cuckoo is sociable and fun — the kind of night where you end up talking to strangers and making plans for the weekend.
