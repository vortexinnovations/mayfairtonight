## The Club That Refuses to Fade

Clubs come and go in Mayfair. Most last a couple of years before the buzz dies and the crowd moves on. [Funky Buddha](/clubs/funky-buddha) is not one of those clubs.

Sitting on Berkeley Street in the heart of Mayfair, Funky Buddha has outlasted dozens of competitors. It's earned a legendary reputation — and not because of marketing. The club delivers. Week after week, year after year.

If you haven't been, this is everything you need to know before you go.

## What's the Vibe?

Funky Buddha is intimate. The room isn't massive, and that's the point. When it fills up on a Saturday night, the energy is compressed and electric. There's nowhere to hide — everyone's part of the atmosphere.

The crowd is a proper mix. Fashion people, creatives, professionals, international visitors. It's not as aggressively exclusive as [Tape London](/clubs/tape-london), but it's not a free-for-all either. You'll need to look the part and ideally be on a guest list or table booking.

The interiors are dark, moody, and effortlessly cool. No gimmicks, no over-the-top theatrics. Just a quality room that knows what it is.

## The Music

This is where Funky Buddha earns its name. The music policy blends hip-hop, RnB, and funky house — and the DJs know how to read the room. Early in the night it's smooth and atmospheric. By 1am the dance floor is heaving and the tempo picks up.

If you're coming from a house music background, you'll appreciate the funky house sets. If you're more hip-hop and RnB, you'll feel right at home. It walks the line better than almost any club in Mayfair.

Don't expect deep underground techno. This is a party club with taste.

## Best Nights to Go

**Saturday** is the headline night. Full capacity, best DJs, peak energy. If you're only going once, go on a Saturday.

**Friday** is excellent too — slightly less packed than Saturday, which some people actually prefer. More room to move, same quality music.

**Wednesday and Thursday** are more low-key but still worth it if you want a quality midweek night. Thursdays in particular draw a good crowd of regulars.

## Dress Code — What to Wear

Smart. Not suits-and-ties smart, but put-together smart. Dark jeans are fine if your shoes and top half are on point. Trainers are a no. Sportswear is a no. If you're unsure, check our [Mayfair dress code guide](/blog/how-to-get-into-mayfair-clubs) — it applies here.

Ladies have more flexibility but the standard is high. Think cocktail bar, not casual pub.

## How to Get In

Here's the thing about Funky Buddha — the door can be tough if you just turn up unannounced. Guest list or a table booking is the smart play.

A few tips that actually work:

- **Arrive before midnight.** The door gets significantly harder after 12.
- **Go as a mixed group.** All-male groups without a booking will struggle. That's just how Mayfair works.
- **Don't argue with the door.** If they say no, they mean no. Be polite, try again another night.

## How It Compares to Other Mayfair Clubs

Funky Buddha occupies a sweet spot in Mayfair. It's more accessible than [Tape London](/clubs/tape-london) but more curated than [Cuckoo Club](/clubs/cuckoo-club). The music is better than most, the crowd is genuinely good, and the atmosphere is consistently on point.

If you want live entertainment, [Reign London](/clubs/reign-london) or [Cirque Le Soir](/clubs/cirque-le-soir) are better picks. If you want something newer and sleeker, check out [Luna Club London](/clubs/luna-club-london). But if you want a proven, reliable, quality night out in Mayfair — Funky Buddha is hard to beat.
